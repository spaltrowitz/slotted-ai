PRIVACY POLICY
Slotted.ai
Last Updated: May 7, 2026

1. INTRODUCTION
Slotted.ai ("we," "us," or "our") respects your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your personal information when you use the Slotted.ai web application at slotted-ai.web.app.

2. INFORMATION WE COLLECT

Account Information:
• Email address, display name, and profile photo (via Google Sign-In through Firebase Auth)

Profile Preferences:
• Timezone
• Social battery level (how social you're feeling)
• Notification preferences

Calendar Data (with your explicit authorization):
• Calendar event titles, start/end times, and locations from connected calendars
• Free/busy status derived from your calendar events
• Calendar sync tokens for incremental updates
• We store free/busy blocks — not full event details — in our database

Social Data:
• Friends list (bidirectional friend relationships)
• Friend preferences and interaction history
• Meetup proposals, RSVPs, and confirmed hangouts
• Meetup activity logs (when, where, with whom, your rating)

Push Notification Tokens:
• Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) device tokens for delivering push notifications

Feedback:
• Any feedback you submit through the app

3. HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION
We use your personal information solely for the following purposes:
• Syncing your calendar availability to find mutual free time with friends
• Displaying when you and your friends are both free
• Creating, managing, and tracking meetup proposals and RSVPs
• Adding confirmed meetups to your connected calendars
• Discovering local events (concerts, shows, community events) relevant to your area
• Sending push notifications for meetup requests, confirmations, and reminders
• Tracking your social activity history (meetup logs)
• Improving the app based on your feedback

4. INFORMATION SHARING AND DISCLOSURE
We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your information only in the following limited circumstances:

• Google Calendar API: We read and write events on calendars you explicitly authorize. Your Google OAuth tokens are encrypted and stored in Supabase Vault. We use webhooks to receive real-time updates when your calendar changes.
• Apple CalDAV (iCloud): If you connect your Apple Calendar, we authenticate via your iCloud app-specific password to read calendar events. Credentials are encrypted in Supabase Vault.
• Microsoft Graph API (Outlook): If you connect Outlook, we use OAuth to read your calendar. Tokens are encrypted in Supabase Vault.
• Ticketmaster, SeatGeek, Eventbrite, Meetup APIs: When you search for events, your search query (location, date, keywords) is sent to these services. No personal identifiers are included in these searches.
• Firebase Cloud Messaging: FCM tokens are used to deliver push notifications to your device. Google processes the delivery.
• Firebase Analytics: We track anonymized usage events (sign-ups, calendar connections, meetup scheduling) prefixed with "slotted_" for product improvement. No personally identifiable information is included in analytics events.
• Friends: Your display name, profile photo, and free/busy availability are visible to people you have accepted as friends. Your calendar event titles and details are NOT shared — only free/busy blocks.
• Legal Requirements: When required by law, regulation, or legal process

5. DATA SECURITY
We implement the following safeguards to protect your information:
• OAuth tokens (Google, Outlook, Apple) are encrypted at rest in Supabase Vault — a dedicated encrypted secrets store
• Supabase Row-Level Security (RLS) ensures users can only access their own data
• Firebase Auth tokens are verified on every API request
• OAuth CSRF protection uses HMAC-signed state tokens with 10-minute TTL
• Rate limiting is applied per IP on all API endpoints
• CORS is restricted to approved origins only
• All data is transmitted over HTTPS
• A predeploy check prevents accidental exposure of localhost URLs in production

6. DATA STORAGE AND RETENTION
• User data, friendships, availability, and meetups are stored in Supabase PostgreSQL with Row-Level Security
• OAuth tokens are stored in Supabase Vault (encrypted at rest)
• FCM tokens are stored in Supabase and removed when you log out or unregister notifications
• We retain your data for as long as your account is active
• You may request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time
• Calendar data is synced incrementally — we store derived free/busy blocks, not raw calendar exports

7. YOUR RIGHTS AND CHOICES
You have the following rights:
• Calendar Control: You choose which calendars to sync and can disconnect any calendar provider at any time
• Friend Visibility: Only accepted friends can see your free/busy availability — you control your friend list
• Push Notifications: You can enable or disable push notifications at any time through your browser settings or in-app
• Access: You may request access to the personal information we have about you
• Correction: You may request correction of inaccurate information
• Deletion: You may request deletion of your account and all associated data, including OAuth tokens and meetup history
• Revoke Calendar Access: You can revoke Google Calendar access via your Google Account settings, Outlook access via Microsoft account settings, or Apple CalDAV by changing your app-specific password

8. CALENDAR DATA
We take special care with calendar data because it reveals your schedule and habits:
• We store free/busy time blocks — NOT full event details, attendee lists, or event descriptions
• Calendar event titles are used only for the sync process and for displaying your own events back to you
• Your friends see only that you are "busy" or "free" during time slots — not what the event is
• Calendar webhook channels are secured with per-user tokens
• You can select exactly which calendars to sync (e.g., work calendar only, personal only, or both)
