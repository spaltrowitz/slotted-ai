## 1. Mission

**Make scheduling a thing of the past.** Take the mental energy out of coordinating social plans so people can spend less time organizing and more time connecting.

## 2. Vision

Give people more control over their social lives — not by adding more tools to manage, but by removing the friction that keeps people from seeing the people they care about.

---

## 3. Problem Statement

Maintaining friendships as a busy adult is hard — not because people don't care, but because scheduling is exhausting. The friction looks like this:

- **Endless back-and-forth texting** — "When are you free?" → "Let me check..." → silence for 3 days
- **Calendar blindness** — You're technically free Saturday, but you forgot. No one reminded you. Plans never happened.
- **Decision fatigue** — Even when you find a time, "What should we do?" kills momentum
- **Guilt and drift** — You haven't seen your college roommate in 4 months. You feel bad but never do anything about it because initiating the scheduling dance feels like work
- **Long-distance neglect** — Friends in other cities fall off entirely because scheduling a call feels like scheduling a meeting

The result: friendships slowly fade — not from lack of caring, but from lack of coordination.

---

## 4. Solution

**Slotted** is an AI-powered social scheduling app that connects to your calendar, understands your preferences, and proactively helps you find time with the people you care about — without the back-and-forth.

### How it works:

1. **Connect your calendar** — Slotted reads your free/busy blocks (never event details) so it knows when you're available
2. **Add your friends** — They connect their calendars too
3. **AI finds the overlap** — Slotted surfaces the best times for you to meet, ranked by your mutual preferences, energy levels, and habits
4. **One-tap to propose** — Send a hangout ping. Your friend accepts, counters, or passes — no group chat required
5. **It gets smarter** — The more you use it, the better it understands when, where, and how you like to hang out

### Key capabilities:

- **Social Battery** — Tell Slotted when you're open to plans, on the fence, or recharging. It respects your energy without broadcasting it to friends.
- **Cadence tracking** — Slotted notices when you haven't seen someone in a while and nudges you before friendships drift
- **Group scheduling** — Select 2+ friends and find times that work for everyone
- **Event discovery** — Browse local events (concerts, shows, sports) and share them with friends as activity inspiration
- **Long-distance support** — Schedule calls and video hangouts across time zones with friends who don't live nearby
- **Works on any device** — PWA that installs like a native app on iOS, Android, and desktop

## 5. What Slotted Is Not

Clarity on scope is just as important as the product itself. Slotted is a social scheduling layer, not a productivity tool or an event platform.

| Slotted is NOT... | Use this instead |
|---|---|
| **A work calendar or meeting scheduler** | Google Calendar, Outlook, Calendly, Cal.com |
| **A platform for large one-off party events with designed virtual invites** | Partiful, Evite, Paperless Post |
| **A group chat app** | iMessage, WhatsApp, GroupMe |
| **A dating app** | Hinge, Bumble |
| **A social media feed** | Instagram, BeReal |
| **A task/project management tool** | Notion, Todoist |

### Important distinction — work meetings:

Slotted does **not** manage, display, or interact with your work meetings. When you connect your Google Calendar, Slotted reads only your free/busy status — it sees that a block is "busy" but never knows it's a standup, a 1:1 with your manager, or a client call. Your work schedule is simply a constraint layer. Slotted sits on top of your existing calendar as a **social planning layer** — it finds the gaps in your life where friendship can happen.

### Important distinction — events:

Slotted helps you discover events and share them with friends as inspiration for hangouts. But it is not an event hosting platform. If you're throwing a birthday party for 50 people and want a beautiful invite with RSVP tracking, use Partiful. Slotted is for the recurring, intimate coordination — the "let's grab coffee," "want to catch that show?", "we haven't hung out in 2 months" moments.

## 6. Value Propositions

### For the individual user:

| Value Prop | Description |
|---|---|
| **Eliminate scheduling friction** | Stop playing calendar tetris over text. Slotted finds mutual availability automatically. |
| **Reduce decision fatigue** | AI suggests when, and event discovery helps with what. Less "I don't know, what do you want to do?" |
| **Maintain relationships without the mental load** | Cadence tracking and gentle nudges keep friendships alive without you having to remember who you haven't seen. |
| **Protect your energy** | Social Battery lets you control when you're available without awkward explanations. No one sees you're "recharging." |
| **Stay connected long-distance** | Timezone-aware call scheduling makes it easy to keep up with friends who moved away. |

### For the friend network:

| Value Prop | Description |
|---|---|
| **Both sides benefit** | Unlike Calendly (one-directional), Slotted is mutual — both friends get scheduling superpowers. |
| **Soft social norms** | "Not this time" instead of "Decline." "Maybe" as a real option. Designed to feel human, not transactional. |
| **Privacy-first** | No one sees your calendar details, your Social Battery, or how busy you are. The AI mediates so no one feels exposed. |

## 7. Target Users

### Primary persona: "The Busy Connector" (age 25–45)

- Technologically savvy professionals who already use digital calendars
- Maintain 5–15 close friendships they wish they were better at nurturing
- Lives in a city (or has friends spread across cities)
- Feels guilty about not seeing people enough but finds scheduling to be the bottleneck
- Values efficiency — if a tool saves them 10 minutes of texting per hangout, they'll use it
- Likely already uses: Google Calendar, iMessage/WhatsApp group chats, maybe Partiful for parties

### Secondary persona: "The Group Coordinator"

- The friend who always ends up organizing the group dinner, the hike, the game night
- Spends disproportionate time in group chats trying to find a time that works
- Would happily delegate scheduling logistics to an app
- Power user who invites others and drives network growth

### Tertiary persona: "The Long-Distance Maintainer"

- Has close friends or family in other cities/time zones
- Wants to stay connected but phone calls feel like meetings to schedule
- Needs timezone-aware call window matching

## 8. Who Slotted Is NOT For

Being clear about who the product isn't for prevents feature creep and sharpens positioning.

| Not our user | Why |
|---|---|
| **People who are fully spontaneous** | If you only make plans by texting "what are you doing right now?", you don't need a scheduling tool. Slotted helps planners, not improvisers. |
| **People who don't use calendars** | Slotted's core value depends on calendar data. If a user doesn't track their time digitally, the AI has nothing to work with. Manual entry exists as a fallback, but the magic is in the sync. |
| **Older adults who don't schedule socially** | If someone's social life is primarily church on Sunday and family dinner on Wednesday — both of which never change — there's no scheduling problem to solve. |
| **People who only see 1–2 people** | If your social circle is tiny and standing plans are easy to maintain, the coordination overhead Slotted eliminates doesn't exist for you. |
| **Event hosts / party planners** | If the goal is a polished invite for a 30-person birthday, Slotted isn't the tool. Use Partiful. Slotted is for the intimate, recurring coordination. |
| **Professional networkers** | Slotted is for real friendships, not "let's grab coffee to pick your brain" scheduling. Use Calendly for that. |

## 9. Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What they do | Where Slotted differs |
|---|---|---|
| **Calendly / Cal.com** | One-directional scheduling (host shares link, guest picks time) | Slotted is mutual and relationship-aware. Both parties are equal. It's social, not transactional. |
| **Doodle / When2meet** | Group polls for finding a single meeting time | Slotted is always-on, not per-event. It continuously knows your availability and suggests proactively. |
| **Google Calendar sharing** | Share full calendars with others | Exposes too much. Slotted shows only free/busy at the AI layer — no event titles, no details leaked. |
| **Partiful** | Beautiful event invites + RSVP for parties | Different use case entirely. Partiful is for events; Slotted is for relationships. Complementary, not competitive. |
| **IRL (app)** | Social calendar / event discovery | Focused on events and public social discovery. Slotted is private, friend-to-friend, calendar-aware. |
| **Group chats (iMessage, WhatsApp)** | The incumbent "scheduler" | Slotted replaces the worst part of group chats (the "when are you free?" thread) while leaving the social chat intact. |

### Slotted's moat:

1. **Calendar data + AI personalization** — The more a user interacts, the smarter suggestions get. This creates switching costs.
2. **Network lock-in** — Your friends are on Slotted. Moving to another tool means re-onboarding your social graph.
3. **Privacy-first design** — Trust is hard to earn and easy to lose. Slotted's architecture (free/busy only, no social signal leakage) is a feature competitors would have to rebuild from scratch.

## 10. Go-to-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Private Beta (Current — Feb/Mar 2026)

- **Audience:** 15–30 friends and family as initial beta testers
- **Goal:** Validate core value prop — does Slotted actually reduce scheduling friction?
- **Mechanics:**
  - Personal invite links shared directly
  - In-app feedback form for bug reports and suggestions
  - Weekly check-ins with most active users
- **Key question to answer:** Do beta users invite their own friends organically, or does growth require pushing?

### Phase 2: User Research Survey (Alongside Beta)

- Send a structured survey to beta testers (friends/family) covering:
  - How often they scheduled hangouts before vs. with Slotted
  - What friction remains
  - Would they recommend it to a friend (NPS)
  - What's missing / what would make them use it daily
- Use the existing [interview guide](05-user-research-interview-guide.md) as a foundation
- Keep it short (5–8 questions, <3 minutes) to maximize response rates

### Phase 3: Expanded Beta via Organic Growth (Apr–Jun 2026)

- **Audience:** Friends-of-friends via invite links — let the network expand naturally
- **Goal:** Test whether the product has organic pull. If users invite friends unprompted, there's signal. If they don't, the value prop needs sharpening.
- **Free marketing channels:**
  - **Reddit** — Post in r/productivity, r/socialskills, r/adulting, r/apps with an honest "I built this" story. Reddit rewards authenticity over polish.
  - **Twitter/X** — Build-in-public thread documenting the journey. The indie hacker community engages with real founder stories.
  - **Product Hunt** — Time a launch for when the product is polished enough for a wider audience. Requires prep (hunter, assets, launch day engagement).
  - **Hacker News (Show HN)** — Technical audience, good for early adopter feedback if the AI angle is interesting.
  - **Local community groups** — Nextdoor, neighborhood Slack/Discord channels, alumni groups
  - **Word of mouth** — The most powerful channel. If the product is genuinely useful, people tell their friends. If they don't, no marketing fixes that.

### Phase 4: Public Launch (Jul–Sep 2026)

- Landing page with waitlist
- Content marketing (blog posts on friendship maintenance, scheduling psychology)
- Targeted communities: climbing gyms, running clubs, book clubs — any group that coordinates recurring meetups

## 11. Business Model

### Current: Free (Beta)

No monetization during beta. The priority is validating product-market fit and building a user base.

### Future monetization options (V2+):

| Model | Description | Viability |
|---|---|---|
| **Freemium** | Free tier: 5 friends, basic scheduling. Pro tier ($3–5/mo): unlimited friends, group scheduling, event discovery, analytics | Most natural fit. The friend limit creates organic upgrade pressure. |
| **Network-based pricing** | Free for individuals, paid for "power coordinators" who schedule for groups frequently | Aligns cost with value — the people who use it most pay. |
| **Affiliate revenue** | Commission on restaurant reservations, event tickets, or activity bookings made through Slotted | Low-friction monetization that adds value rather than restricting features. |
| **SMS/notification tier** | Free: push notifications. Pro: SMS reminders for friends not on the app | SMS has real costs (~$0.01/message). Passing this to power users is fair. |

### What we will NOT do:

- **Sell user data** — Non-negotiable. Calendar data and social graphs are deeply personal.
- **Show ads** — Breaks the intimate, trust-based experience.
- **Charge per transaction** — Scheduling a hangout should never feel like it costs money.

## 13. On Organic Growth & Your Beta Strategy

> *"Is it fair to think it would pick up on its own if it's useful, otherwise it shows me that there isn't a need for it?"*

**Partially yes, with caveats.**

**The yes:** For a social scheduling tool, organic growth (users inviting friends) is the most honest signal of product-market fit. Slotted is inherently a network product — it's useless alone. If someone finds it valuable, they *must* invite friends to get value from it. So if no one invites anyone, that's a real signal that the pain isn't acute enough.

**The caveats:**

1. **Cold start problem is real.** The first user in a friend group gets zero value until a friend joins. You need to seed complete friend clusters (invite a group of 4–5 friends together, not individuals). A solo user will churn before ever experiencing the magic.

2. **"Useful" and "useful enough to change behavior" are different.** People might agree scheduling is annoying but not annoying enough to adopt a new tool. The bar isn't "is this nice?" — it's "is this worth the effort of getting my friends to sign up?"

3. **Distribution ≠ product quality.** Many great products failed because distribution was unsolved. If Slotted is genuinely useful but growth is slow, it might mean your *onboarding-to-value* pipeline is broken (too many steps before the "aha" moment), not that the product is bad.

4. **Give it a real runway.** Don't judge in 2 weeks. Social products need 2–3 months to see if behavior sticks. Set a clear evaluation point (e.g., "by June 2026, have 5 organic invites happened?") and commit to iterating until then.

**Recommendation:** Seed 3–4 complete friend clusters in beta. If even one cluster adopts Slotted as their default scheduling method, that's strong signal. If none do after 3 months of iteration, the problem may not be painful enough for this solution.

## 14. User Research Survey for Beta Testers

**Yes, you should absolutely send a survey.** You have a captive, friendly audience — take advantage of it. Here's a lean survey design:

### Recommended survey (5 questions, <2 minutes):

1. **Before Slotted, how did you typically schedule hangouts with friends?** (Multi-select: group chat, text back-and-forth, shared calendar, just show up, other)

2. **How many times have you used Slotted to schedule or propose a hangout?** (0 / 1–2 / 3–5 / 5+)

3. **What's the #1 thing that would make you use Slotted more?** (Free text — this is where the gold is)

4. **How likely are you to recommend Slotted to a friend?** (0–10 NPS scale)

5. **What almost stopped you from using it?** (Free text — surfaces friction and objections)

### When to send:

- After users have had the app for **at least 2 weeks** (enough time to try scheduling at least once)
- Use a simple tool: Google Forms or Typeform (free tier)
- Follow up personally with anyone who gives detailed feedback — that's your future power user

## 15. Major Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| **"This takes the spontaneity out of seeing friends"** | Slotted doesn't replace spontaneity — it handles the *other* type of plan. The "we should hang out soon" that turns into 2 months of nothing. Spontaneous hangs still happen naturally. Slotted catches the ones that wouldn't happen otherwise. |
| **"I don't want to share my calendar"** | You're not sharing your calendar. Slotted sees free/busy only — no event names, no details, no attendees. Your friends never see your schedule. The AI mediates everything privately. There's also a manual entry mode if you prefer. |
| **"My friends won't sign up"** | This is the biggest real objection. Slotted mitigates it with invite links (one-tap signup), SMS bridge for non-users (V1 planned), and by making the first experience frictionless. But ultimately, the product must be good enough that inviting a friend feels like doing them a favor, not asking them for one. |
| **"Another app? I have too many already"** | Fair. Slotted is a PWA — no App Store download, installs from the browser in 2 taps. And it replaces a behavior you're already doing (texting "when are you free?"), not adding a new one. |
| **"I'll just text my friends"** | You will! And that's fine for simple plans. Slotted is for when texting fails — the 4-person dinner that takes 47 messages to schedule, the friend you keep meaning to see but never actually coordinate with. |
| **"Scheduling apps feel impersonal / corporate"** | That's Calendly. Slotted is designed with soft social language ("Not this time" vs. "Decline"), no visible status judgments, and warm nudges — not meeting invites. It should feel like a thoughtful friend, not an HR tool. |
| **"What about my privacy?"** | Slotted was designed privacy-first from day one. Friends never see your calendar details, Social Battery status, or how busy you are. There are no social judgments encoded (no "group-only" friend labels). The AI uses everyone's data to generate suggestions but never exposes raw inputs. Data is encrypted, never sold, and you can delete your account at any time. |

## 16. Honest Assessment — Flaws & Risks

You asked for a holistic look at the flaws. Here they are, ranked by severity:

### Critical risks:

**1. The Cold Start / Chicken-and-Egg Problem**
This is the existential risk. Slotted is useless with zero friends on it. Every new user faces a moment where they've signed up, connected their calendar... and have no one to schedule with. If the path from "I signed up" to "I scheduled a hangout" is too long or requires too much evangelism, users churn before experiencing value. *Mitigation: Seed complete friend clusters. Make inviting feel effortless. Consider a "solo value" feature (personal social insights, event discovery) that delivers value even before friends join.*

**2. Behavior Change is Brutally Hard**
People have been scheduling via text for 15+ years. Even if Slotted is better, "good enough" is the enemy of adoption. The switching cost isn't money — it's habit. You're not just asking someone to use an app; you're asking them to change how they coordinate with every friend. *Mitigation: Don't try to replace texting entirely. Position Slotted as the tool you open when texting has failed ("we've been saying 'let's hang out' for 6 weeks — let me just Slot us in").*

**3. Network Effects Cut Both Ways**
Network effects are powerful when growing — but they also mean that if a user's friends leave, the user leaves too. One disengaged person in a friend cluster can collapse the whole group's usage. *Mitigation: Ensure value persists even with partial adoption. If 3 out of 5 friends use it, it should still be useful for that subset.*

### Significant risks:

**4. Calendar Permission Anxiety**
Despite clear privacy messaging, some people viscerally recoil at granting calendar access. "Free/busy only" sounds reasonable to you as the builder — but to a user, "this app wants to access my Google Calendar" triggers the same alarm bells as any other permission request. *Mitigation: Manual entry mode exists as a fallback. Consider showing a "here's exactly what we see" preview during onboarding.*

**5. The "Organizer Tax"**
Slotted may disproportionately benefit the person who initiates plans. The friend who receives a ping gets value passively, but may not see enough reason to stay engaged or open the app proactively. If Slotted becomes "the app my organized friend uses to schedule me," it's sticky for one person but disposable for the other. *Mitigation: Ensure the receiving side gets value too — nudges about friends they haven't seen, event discovery, cadence awareness.*

**6. AI Suggestions Must Be Good Early**
The AI learns from behavior, but early on it has no data. If the first 3 suggestions are bad times (Tuesday at 7am for drinks), users lose trust fast and don't come back. Rule-based logic must be solid before ML has enough signal. *Mitigation: Onboarding preferences (preferred times, Social Battery) give the algorithm a strong starting point. Invest heavily in the rule-based fallback.*

**7. No Revenue Model Yet**
Running the app costs money (Supabase, Firebase, API calls) and currently generates none. At small scale this is fine, but if the app grows, costs grow with it — calendar syncs, push notifications, event API calls. You need a monetization plan before you need money, not after. *Mitigation: The freemium model (free tier with friend limit, paid for unlimited) is the most natural fit. Plan it now, implement it when there's demand.*

### Minor risks:

**8. Platform Limitations**
PWAs on iOS still have limitations (push notifications are better now but not perfect, no background sync). Power users may expect native app behavior that a PWA can't deliver. *Mitigation: PWA is the right V1 choice. Native apps are planned for V2 if demand warrants it.*

**9. Google Calendar Dependency**
V1 only supports Google Calendar. Users on Outlook or Apple Calendar are second-class citizens with manual entry. In some professional circles (finance, law, consulting), Outlook is dominant. *Mitigation: Apple Calendar (CalDAV) is partially built. Outlook integration is planned for V2. The addressable market with Google Calendar alone is large enough for beta.*

**10. Feature Scope vs. Polish**
The MVP has a lot of features (events, groups, notifications, hangout logging, cadence tracking, Social Battery). Breadth is impressive but risks none of them being truly polished. A scheduling app that does 15 things at 70% quality may lose to one that does 3 things at 100%. *Mitigation: Identify the 3 features that drive the most value in beta (likely: calendar sync, availability matching, scheduling ping) and polish those relentlessly. Let the rest be "nice to have" that you improve based on feedback.*

## 19. Summary

Slotted exists because adult friendships shouldn't require a project manager. The scheduling problem is real, validated by every "we should hang out soon" text that never turns into plans. The product is designed with taste — privacy-first, socially soft, AI-mediated — and the MVP is comprehensive enough to test the full thesis.

The biggest risk isn't competition or technology. It's whether the pain of scheduling is acute enough to drive adoption of a new tool, and whether friend clusters will adopt it together. The beta period is the test. Seed it intentionally, measure organic growth honestly, and let the data tell you whether to accelerate or pivot.

**If people invite their friends, you have something. If they don't, no amount of marketing will fix it.**
